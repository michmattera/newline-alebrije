"La flor de cempasúchil "
A yellow flower very common in Mexico, open just on that day.

Make an altar!

Altar

An altar is a place where you make a connection with the other world. You do it using the four elements, like the elements of the alebrijes: Air, Fire, Earth, and Water. It is usually in your house, and is divided in three different levels that represent : Heaven, Earth and the World of the Death. I am going to show how to make an altar in different steps or if you want you can follow the video below!

  1. Base of the altar

    The base on the altar is what you are going to use to prepare the altar in your house or where you have decided to wait for your alebrije.
    You can simply use a table or a desk for the first and central level.
    To do the top level you can place any object that is rigid and can sostein weight as for example a box or a few books together.
    The third and bottom level you can use a box or even a old laggage will be okay.
    The most important thing is that all the surface are flat. Now you can cover all the surface with what you have in your house, just to not see the difference of level under it. You can either use a blanket or you can buy something related with the day of the death!
    Just have fun and use something you like!

  2. La flor de cempasúchil

    This flower , which grow mostly in October and November according to traditions,is also called the flower of the dead, flower of the twenty petals or cempasuchil flower.
    It is considered since pre-Hispanic times as a symbol of life and death.
    You are going to need this flower and the petals of the cempasúchil flower to create paths and guide the alebrije and the deceased to the altar that you are building.
    It is a flower that connects the open portal from the realm of the death to the mortal world to your house.
    That is why you need this flower in your altar and why is so important.
    You can place the flowers a bit everywhere in the altar but the important part is that you make a path with the petals from the altar for at least 1 meter.
